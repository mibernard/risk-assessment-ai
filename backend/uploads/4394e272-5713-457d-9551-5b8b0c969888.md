# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

**Document Version:** 2.1  
**Effective Date:** January 2024  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

## 1. Executive Summary

This policy establishes mandatory Anti-Money Laundering (AML) controls for all financial transactions processed by our institution. All employees must comply with these requirements to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing.

## 2. Transaction Thresholds

### 2.1 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

All transactions meeting the following criteria require Enhanced Due Diligence:

- **Large Transactions:** Any single transaction or series of related transactions exceeding **$10,000 USD**
- **High-Risk Countries:** Any transaction regardless of amount involving designated high-risk jurisdictions
- **Structured Transactions:** Multiple transactions appearing designed to evade reporting thresholds

### 2.2 Standard Due Diligence (SDD)

Transactions between $3,000 and $10,000 require standard verification procedures including:

- Customer identity verification
- Purpose of transaction documentation
- Source of funds declaration

## 3. High-Risk Jurisdictions

The following jurisdictions are classified as **HIGH RISK** for money laundering and require additional scrutiny:

### Tier 1 - Maximum Scrutiny

- Countries under comprehensive sanctions
- Jurisdictions with weak regulatory frameworks
- Nations identified by FATF as having strategic AML deficiencies

**Examples include but are not limited to:**

- Iran
- North Korea
- Myanmar
- Countries with ongoing conflicts affecting financial oversight

### Tier 2 - Enhanced Monitoring

- Offshore financial centers with limited transparency
- Jurisdictions with bank secrecy laws
- Countries with limited international cooperation

## 4. Suspicious Activity Indicators

### 4.1 Red Flags

Transactions exhibiting these characteristics must be reported within 24 hours:

1. **Structuring:** Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds
2. **Rapid Movement:** Funds deposited and immediately transferred overseas
3. **Unusual Patterns:** Activity inconsistent with customer profile
4. **Third-Party Payments:** Payments to unrelated parties without clear business purpose
5. **Complex Routing:** Unnecessarily complicated transaction paths through multiple jurisdictions

### 4.2 Customer Behavior Warning Signs

- Customer reluctant to provide information
- Customer has no clear economic rationale for transaction
- Customer relationship involves sanctioned entities or persons
- Customer requests unusual confidentiality or speed

## 5. Reporting Requirements

### 5.1 Internal Reporting

- All suspicious activities must be reported to the Compliance Officer within **24 hours**
- Use the internal SAR (Suspicious Activity Report) form
- Do not notify the customer that a report has been filed

### 5.2 Regulatory Reporting

- File SARs with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) within **30 days** of detection
- Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs) required for cash transactions exceeding $10,000
- Cross-border wire transfers exceeding $3,000 must be reported with complete originator information

## 6. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 6.1 Minimum Requirements

Before establishing a business relationship, verify:

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Government-issued photo identification
- Tax identification number
- Purpose and intended nature of the business relationship

### 6.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Required for:

- **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs):** Current or former government officials, senior executives at state-owned enterprises
- **High Net Worth Individuals:** Customers with assets exceeding $5 million
- **High-Risk Industries:** Casinos, money service businesses, precious metals dealers

EDD includes:

- Senior management approval for establishing relationship
- Source of wealth verification
- Source of funds verification for each transaction
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring with periodic review

## 7. Record Keeping

All AML-related records must be retained for a minimum of **7 years** including:

- Customer identification documents
- Transaction records
- SAR filings and supporting documentation
- Training records
- Risk assessments

## 8. Training Requirements

- All employees handling customer transactions must complete AML training annually
- New employees must complete training within 30 days of hire
- Specialized training required for compliance staff and senior management

## 9. Sanctions Screening

### 9.1 Real-Time Screening

All transactions must be screened against:

- OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control) sanctions lists
- UN Security Council sanctions lists
- EU sanctions lists
- Local regulatory authority lists

### 9.2 Screening Frequency

- Customer screening: At onboarding and quarterly thereafter
- Transaction screening: Real-time before processing
- Watch list updates: Implemented within 24 hours of release

## 10. Penalties for Non-Compliance

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Regulatory fines up to **$1,000,000 per violation**
- Criminal prosecution of responsible individuals
- License revocation
- Reputational damage
- Employee disciplinary action up to and including termination

## 11. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed to reflect:

- Changes in regulatory requirements
- Emerging money laundering typologies
- Internal audit findings
- Risk assessment results
