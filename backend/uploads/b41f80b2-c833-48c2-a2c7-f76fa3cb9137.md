# Know Your Customer (KYC) Guidelines

**Document Version:** 3.0  
**Effective Date:** January 2024  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

## 1. Purpose

These guidelines establish comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures to ensure proper customer identification, risk assessment, and ongoing monitoring in compliance with applicable regulations.

## 2. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

### 2.1 Individual Customers

**Minimum Required Information:**
- Full legal name (as appears on government ID)
- Date of birth
- Residential address (P.O. Box not acceptable as primary address)
- Identification number (SSN, passport, national ID)
- Occupation and employer
- Expected account activity
- Source of funds

**Acceptable Identification Documents:**
- Passport (unexpired)
- Driver's license (government-issued)
- National identity card
- Permanent resident card

### 2.2 Corporate Customers

**Required Documentation:**
- Certificate of incorporation
- Business license
- Articles of association
- Board resolution authorizing account opening
- List of beneficial owners (25% ownership or more)
- List of authorized signatories
- Financial statements (last 2 years)
- Business plan or description of activities

### 2.3 Beneficial Ownership

For all corporate customers, identify and verify:
- **All individuals** owning 25% or more of equity
- **One individual** with significant control or management authority
- Use of intermediaries, nominees, or bearer shares must be justified

## 3. Risk-Based Approach

### 3.1 Risk Rating Methodology

Assign customer risk ratings based on multiple factors:

**LOW RISK (Score: 0-30)**
- Domestic customer in low-risk jurisdiction
- Transparent ownership structure
- Well-established business with strong reputation
- Low transaction volumes (<$50,000 monthly)
- Simple product usage

**MEDIUM RISK (Score: 31-60)**
- Customer from medium-risk jurisdiction
- Moderate transaction volumes ($50,000-$250,000 monthly)
- Use of intermediary products
- Limited operating history (2-5 years)
- Cash-intensive business

**HIGH RISK (Score: 61-100)**
- Customer from high-risk jurisdiction
- Complex ownership structure
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) status
- High transaction volumes (>$250,000 monthly)
- Industries prone to money laundering:
  - Money service businesses
  - Casinos and gaming
  - Precious metals/stones dealers
  - Real estate
  - Arms dealers
  - Offshore companies

### 3.2 Risk Scoring Matrix

| Factor | Low Risk (0-10) | Medium Risk (11-20) | High Risk (21-30) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Jurisdiction | USA, Canada, EU, Australia | Most other countries | High-risk FATF list, sanctioned countries |
| Customer Type | Individual, Listed Company | SME, Established Private Company | Offshore entity, PEP, Shell company |
| Products/Services | Basic banking | Lending, Trade Finance | International wires, Private banking |
| Transaction Volume | <$50K/month | $50K-$250K/month | >$250K/month |
| Geographic Reach | Domestic only | Regional | Global, High-risk countries |

## 4. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

### 4.1 When EDD is Required

- Customer risk rating ≥ 61 (HIGH)
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Non-face-to-face customers
- Correspondent banking relationships
- Private banking customers

### 4.2 EDD Procedures

**Additional Information Requirements:**
- Detailed source of wealth statement with supporting documentation
- Source of funds for each significant transaction
- References from other financial institutions
- Adverse media screening
- Enhanced background checks
- Understanding of customer's business model

**Approval Requirements:**
- Senior management (VP level or above) must approve relationship
- Annual review by compliance officer required
- Transaction monitoring thresholds set at lower levels

### 4.3 PEP Identification

**Politically Exposed Persons include:**
- Heads of state or government
- Senior politicians and political party officials
- Senior government, judicial, or military officials
- Senior executives of state-owned enterprises
- Important political party officials
- **Family members** and **close associates** of the above

**PEP Verification:**
- Screen against PEP databases at onboarding
- Re-screen quarterly
- Obtain senior management approval for relationship
- Apply enhanced monitoring

## 5. Ongoing Monitoring

### 5.1 Monitoring Frequency

**HIGH RISK Customers:**
- Quarterly account review
- Real-time transaction monitoring with low thresholds
- Annual refresh of KYC information
- Immediate alert on adverse media

**MEDIUM RISK Customers:**
- Semi-annual account review
- Transaction monitoring with standard thresholds
- Biennial KYC refresh

**LOW RISK Customers:**
- Annual account review
- Transaction monitoring with elevated thresholds
- KYC refresh every 3 years

### 5.2 Triggers for Immediate Review

- Significant increase in transaction volume (>50% vs baseline)
- Change in transaction patterns
- Transactions involving high-risk countries
- Customer behavior inconsistent with profile
- Adverse media or sanctions list match
- Customer unwilling to provide requested information

## 6. Customer Exit Strategy

### 6.1 Grounds for Termination

Relationship must be terminated if:
- Customer refuses to provide required KYC information
- False or misleading information discovered
- Customer appears on sanctions list
- Repeated suspicious activity
- Regulatory direction
- Risk profile no longer acceptable

### 6.2 Termination Process

1. Obtain senior management approval
2. Document reasons for termination
3. File SAR if suspicious activity involved
4. Provide customer with required notice (per contract and regulations)
5. Return remaining funds via verifiable method
6. Maintain records for required retention period

## 7. Documentation Requirements

### 7.1 Records to Maintain

- Original or certified copies of identification documents
- Risk assessment and scoring documentation
- Customer communications
- Transaction records
- Periodic review documentation
- Source of funds/wealth verification

### 7.2 Retention Period

- **Minimum 7 years** from relationship termination
- May be longer depending on jurisdiction
- Electronic storage acceptable if secure and searchable

## 8. Red Flags - Unusual Customer Behavior

Alert compliance immediately if customer:
- Provides minimal or suspicious information
- Shows uncommon concern for secrecy
- Has no clear business purpose for services
- Makes frequent changes to beneficiaries
- Transacts with known shell companies or tax havens
- Conducts transactions inconsistent with stated business
- Appears coached or overly rehearsed
- Shows unusual knowledge of law enforcement techniques
- Requests services outside normal business model

## 9. Training Requirements

### 9.1 Frequency
- Annual KYC training for all customer-facing staff
- Specialized training for compliance and risk staff
- Updates provided whenever regulations change

### 9.2 Content
- Customer identification procedures
- Risk assessment methodology
- Red flag recognition
- Escalation procedures
- Regulatory requirements
- Case studies

## 10. Quality Assurance

### 10.1 Internal Audit
- Annual audit of KYC program effectiveness
- Sample testing of customer files
- Review of risk rating accuracy
- Assessment of staff competency

### 10.2 Independent Testing
- External audit every 2 years
- Regulatory examination readiness
- Benchmark against industry best practices

## 11. Technology and Tools

### 11.1 KYC Systems
- Customer screening software with sanctions/PEP lists
- Document verification tools
- Risk scoring calculators
- Transaction monitoring systems
- Case management platforms

### 11.2 Data Sources
- Global sanctions databases (OFAC, UN, EU)
- PEP databases (World-Check, Dow Jones, etc.)
- Adverse media screening services
- Corporate registry databases
- Credit bureaus

## 12. Exceptions and Escalation

### 12.1 Exception Process
- Document reason for exception
- Obtain Compliance Officer approval
- Enhanced monitoring required
- Periodic review of exception

### 12.2 Escalation Path
1. Relationship Manager → Team Lead
2. Team Lead → Compliance Officer
3. Compliance Officer → Chief Compliance Officer
4. Chief Compliance Officer → Board (for material issues)
