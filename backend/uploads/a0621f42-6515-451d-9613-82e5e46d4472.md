# Sanctions Compliance Policy

**Document Version:** 2.3  
**Effective Date:** January 2024  
**Classification:** Confidential

## 1. Introduction

This policy establishes mandatory procedures for sanctions screening and compliance to prevent transactions with sanctioned individuals, entities, and countries. Non-compliance may result in severe civil and criminal penalties.

## 2. Regulatory Framework

### 2.1 Applicable Sanctions Programs

Our institution complies with all sanctions programs administered by:

- **OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)** - United States
- **UN Security Council** - United Nations
- **EU Council** - European Union
- **HM Treasury** - United Kingdom
- **Local regulatory authorities**

### 2.2 Types of Sanctions

**Comprehensive Sanctions:**
- Complete prohibition on transactions with targeted countries
- Applies to all goods, services, and financial transactions
- Currently applies to: Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea region

**Targeted Sanctions:**
- Specific individuals or entities designated (SDN List)
- Sectoral sanctions (specific industries)
- Asset freezes and transaction blocks

## 3. Screening Requirements

### 3.1 Mandatory Screening Points

**Customer Onboarding:**
- Screen all new customers against sanctions lists
- Screen beneficial owners and authorized signatories
- Screen related parties and intermediaries
- Document screening results in customer file

**Transaction Processing:**
- Real-time screening before processing
- Screen all parties: originator, beneficiary, intermediaries
- Screen payment reference fields for entity names
- Automated blocking of matches

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
- Daily batch screening of entire customer base
- Immediate screening when sanctions lists updated
- Re-screen when customer information changes

### 3.2 Screening Scope

Screen against the following lists:
- **OFAC SDN (Specially Designated Nationals)**
- **OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List**
- **UN Security Council Sanctions List**
- **EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List**
- **UK HM Treasury Financial Sanctions List**
- **Country-specific sectoral sanctions lists**
- **PEP lists** (supplementary screening)

## 4. High-Risk Countries and Jurisdictions

### 4.1 Prohibited Jurisdictions

**NO transactions permitted** with entities or persons in:
- **Iran:** Comprehensive sanctions (limited humanitarian exceptions)
- **North Korea:** Comprehensive sanctions (no exceptions)
- **Syria:** Comprehensive sanctions (limited humanitarian exceptions)
- **Crimea/Sevastopol:** Comprehensive sanctions
- **Donetsk/Luhansk:** Comprehensive sanctions

### 4.2 Restricted Jurisdictions

Enhanced scrutiny required for transactions involving:
- **Cuba:** Limited transactions permitted, OFAC license required
- **Venezuela:** Sectoral sanctions on petroleum, financial sectors
- **Belarus:** Targeted sanctions on specific entities and sectors
- **Myanmar:** Targeted sanctions on military entities
- **Russia:** Sectoral sanctions and targeted designations

### 4.3 Country Risk Assessment

**Tier 1 - Prohibited:**
- Comprehensive sanctions apply
- All transactions blocked
- No business relationships permitted

**Tier 2 - High Restrictions:**
- Sectoral sanctions in place
- Requires senior management approval
- Enhanced compliance procedures
- Regular monitoring

**Tier 3 - Enhanced Scrutiny:**
- Limited sanctions or high risk for violations
- Additional due diligence required
- Compliance officer review

## 5. Screening Process

### 5.1 Name Screening Algorithm

**Matching Criteria:**
- 100% match → **Automatic block**
- 95-99% match → **Manual review required**
- 85-94% match → **Enhanced review**
- <85% match → **Clear** (document decision)

**Factors to Consider:**
- Name spelling variations
- Aliases and known associates
- Address matches
- Date of birth (for individuals)
- ID numbers (passport, tax ID)
- Entity type and structure

### 5.2 False Positive Management

**Review Process:**
1. Verify customer/transaction details
2. Compare against sanctions list entry details
3. Check for distinguishing information (DOB, address, ID)
4. Document decision rationale
5. Obtain supervisor approval for clearing

**Common False Positives:**
- Common names (e.g., Muhammad Ali, John Smith)
- Incomplete customer data
- Similar company names
- Historical matches (removed from lists)

## 6. Hit Management

### 6.1 Confirmed Match - Immediate Actions

If sanctions match confirmed:
1. **BLOCK transaction immediately** - Do not process
2. **FREEZE any assets** held for the party
3. **Notify Compliance Officer** within 1 hour
4. **Do NOT notify the customer** of the block
5. **File blocked property report** with OFAC within 10 days

### 6.2 Reporting Requirements

**To OFAC:**
- Blocked Property Report - within 10 business days
- Annual Report of Blocked Property - by September 30
- Voluntary self-disclosure if violation discovered

**To EU Authorities:**
- Immediate notification of frozen funds
- Quarterly reporting on frozen assets
- Annual compliance attestation

### 6.3 Asset Freeze Procedures

When assets must be frozen:
- Immediately block all accounts and transactions
- Segregate frozen assets
- Maintain detailed records of frozen amounts
- Reject any incoming payments for beneficiary
- Do not release or transfer funds without regulatory approval

## 7. Wire Transfer Requirements

### 7.1 SWIFT Message Screening

**Mandatory Checks:**
- Originator name and address (field 50)
- Beneficiary name and address (field 59)
- Intermediary banks (fields 52, 53, 54)
- Ultimate creditor (field 58) if present
- Payment details and references (field 70)

### 7.2 Cover Payments

Special attention for cover payments:
- Screen underlying beneficiary information
- Verify correspondent bank compliance
- Enhanced review for high-risk corridors
- Document decision trail

### 7.3 Suspicious Routing

**Red Flags:**
- Unnecessarily complex payment chains
- Routing through multiple high-risk jurisdictions
- Use of obscure correspondent banks
- Inconsistent beneficiary information
- Payment amounts split across multiple transactions

## 8. Sectoral Sanctions

### 8.1 Russia Sectoral Sanctions

**Directive 1 - Financial Services (SSI List):**
- Prohibited: Debt >14 days maturity, equity
- Applies to designated Russian financial institutions
- Requires identification of 50%+ ownership

**Directive 2 - Energy Sector:**
- Prohibited: Debt >60 days maturity for Russian energy companies
- Enhanced scrutiny on oil and gas industry dealings

**Directive 3 - Defense Sector:**
- Prohibited: All dealings with Russian defense/intelligence entities
- Includes goods, technology, services

**Directive 4 - Technology Sector:**
- Restricted exports of certain technologies to Russia
- Requires export license verification

### 8.2 Due Diligence for Sectoral

- Screen for 50%+ ownership by designated entities
- Identify beneficial ownership structures
- Review nature of goods/services
- Verify transaction does not violate directives
- Document compliance determination

## 9. Export Controls and Trade Sanctions

### 9.1 Dual-Use Goods

Enhanced scrutiny for transactions involving:
- Technology with military applications
- Encryption software/hardware
- Aerospace components
- Nuclear materials and technology
- Chemical/biological materials

### 9.2 Licensing Requirements

Verify proper licenses for:
- Exports to sanctioned countries
- Items on Commerce Control List (CCL)
- Defense articles (ITAR)
- Dual-use technologies

## 11. Consequences of Violations

### 11.1 Civil Penalties

**OFAC Penalties:**
- Up to $311,562 per violation or 2x transaction value
- Strict liability (no intent required)
- Penalties apply to facilitating violations

**EU Penalties:**
- Unlimited fines at member state discretion
- Asset seizure
- Loss of trading privileges

### 11.2 Criminal Penalties

For willful violations:
- Fines up to $1,000,000 for corporations
- Fines up to $50,000 for individuals
- Imprisonment up to 20 years
- Loss of export privileges

### 11.3 Reputational Risk

- Adverse media coverage
- Loss of correspondent banking relationships
- Customer attrition
- Regulatory scrutiny and audits

## 12. Recordkeeping

### 12.1 Required Records

Maintain for minimum 7 years:
- Screening results (hits and clears)
- Investigation documentation
- Blocking reports
- License applications and approvals
- Training records
- Policy and procedure updates
- Sanctions list version logs

### 12.2 Audit Trail

System must capture:
- Date and time of screening
- Lists screened against
- User performing screening
- Match results
- Decision and approver
- Supporting documentation

## 13. Governance and Oversight

### 13.1 Sanctions Compliance Officer

Responsibilities:
- Oversee sanctions program
- Monitor regulatory changes
- Conduct risk assessments
- Review screening escalations
- File regulatory reports
- Liaise with authorities

### 13.2 Board Reporting

Quarterly reporting to Board/Senior Management:
- Program effectiveness metrics
- Screening statistics (hits, false positives)
- Regulatory developments
- Training completion rates
- Audit findings and remediation

## 14. Remediation and Self-Disclosure

### 14.1 If Violation Discovered

1. **Stop the violation immediately**
2. **Secure all related records**
3. **Notify legal counsel**
4. **Conduct internal investigation**
5. **Consider voluntary self-disclosure** to OFAC
6. **Implement corrective actions**

### 14.2 Voluntary Self-Disclosure Benefits

- Reduced penalties (typically 50% reduction)
- Demonstrates good faith
- Limits reputational damage
- Maintains regulatory relationship
